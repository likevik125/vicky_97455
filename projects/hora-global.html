HoraGlobal WorkForce OS

A workforce operations dashboard for distributed teams.

Overview

A workforce management dashboard covering scheduling, attendance, payroll, and reporting for distributed teams. Designed to replace three disconnected tools with one calm, data-dense operating surface.

Problem

Operations leads were stitching together spreadsheets, chat, and a legacy HR tool to run the day. Data was stale, actions were slow, and mistakes were expensive.

Goals

  • Consolidate three tools into a single daily surface.
  • Make the most-used actions reachable in one click.
  • Present dense data without feeling overwhelming.
  • Support role-based views for admins, managers, and staff.

Role

  • Owned IA, interaction design, and visual system.
  • Ran stakeholder interviews and JTBD workshops.
  • Designed the token system and reusable data components.
  • Prepared developer specs and edge-case documentation.

Target users

Operations leads and HR managers running 20–500 person distributed teams.

Research

Six stakeholder interviews and two workflow shadow sessions. Surfaced that 70% of daily time was spent inside three specific views — those became the design's centre of gravity.

Personas

Ravi

Operations Lead

Needs

Live headcount, quick shift edits, exportable reports.

Frustrations

Switching tools, waiting on data, manual reconciliation.

Meera

HR Manager

Needs

Approvals queue, policy visibility, audit trails.

Frustrations

Losing context across tools, unclear approval status.

Pain points

  • Scheduling changes required three separate updates.
  • Reports lived in exports, not the product.
  • Approvals were buried in email threads.

Information architecture

A left-rail navigation grouped by role, with a persistent command bar for search and quick actions. Every view exposes filters, saved segments, and export.

Competitive analysis

Reviewed Deel, Rippling, and Zoho People for pattern inspiration; leaned into Linear's density and keyboard-first ergonomics for the interaction model.

Design decisions

  • Command bar (⌘K) for actions and navigation.
  • Inline editing on tables to remove modal detours.
  • Saved views replace repeated filter setup.
  • Approvals surface as a queue, not scattered inbox items.

Wireframes

Wireframes focused on the three highest-traffic views. Data density was tested with realistic rows before any visual design.

Visual design

A calm, neutral surface with a single warm accent for interactive affordances. Tables use restrained rules and consistent alignment to make scanning effortless.

Design system

Tokenised type, spacing, and colour; a data-table pattern with variants for density, selection, and inline edit; a form kit tuned for admin workflows.

Prototype

Clickable prototype of the daily loop — dashboard → approvals → report — used for exec review.

Accessibility

  • Full keyboard navigation with visible focus.
  • Contrast validated on data-dense tables.
  • Motion respects reduced-motion preference.

Developer handoff

Spec pack with token definitions, component states, and interaction notes. Paired review with engineering on data-table behaviour and empty states.

Challenges

  • Balancing density with legibility on dashboard views.
  • Designing role-based UI without three separate products.
  • Making bulk actions feel safe.

Solutions

  • Consistent row rhythm and restrained colour.
  • Progressive disclosure of role-specific controls.
  • Undo + confirmation patterns tuned to action weight.

Results

  • One unified surface replaced three tools in daily flow.
  • Common actions reduced to a single click or shortcut.
  • Design system unblocked parallel engineering streams.

Impact

The operations lead persona could complete their morning loop without leaving the dashboard — the primary success metric agreed with stakeholders.

Key learnings

  • Dense products win on rhythm, not decoration.
  • Command bars remove more UI than they add.
  • Design tokens are the fastest way to align a team.

Future improvements

  • AI-assisted scheduling suggestions.
  • Native mobile companion for managers.
  • Deeper payroll integrations.
