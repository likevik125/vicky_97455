SmartFood Safe

An AI-powered B2B enterprise SaaS platform that digitizes and centralizes supplier quality assurance, replacing manual spreadsheet bottlenecks with real-time risk analytics and compliance ledgers.

Overview

SmartFood Safe is a next-generation B2B Enterprise SaaS platform that digitizes and centralizes supplier quality assurance. It replaces manual friction with data-driven workflows, establishing a transparent, real-time bridge between enterprise Quality Assurance (QA) teams and global suppliers to resolve deviations instantly.

Problem

Fragmented Communication Loops & The Spreadsheet Bottleneck: When a food safety defect or lab deviation occurs, internal Quality Assurance (QA) teams, procurement managers, and external suppliers communicate via scattered email threads, phone calls, and personal messaging apps. Critical details get lost, causing delays in resolving urgent safety threats.

Furthermore, vendor directories, risk metrics, and regulatory compliance documents are managed across massive, disconnected spreadsheets. This static data ecosystem offers zero real-time visibility, making it nearly impossible to spot regional risk patterns before they compromise a production line.

Goals

  • Establish an Immutable Source of Truth: Bring internal QA teams and global suppliers into a centralized portal to consolidate chat histories, dispute records, and lab evidence into a single audit-ready hub.
  • Unlock Real-Time Visual Intelligence: Translate dense relational data into actionable command centers using interactive features like network-wide risk heatmaps and automated SLA countdown timers.
  • Deploy Automated Preventive Guardrails: Introduce intelligent tracking ledgers that continuously monitor global safety credentials and trigger automated warnings weeks before certifications lapse.
  • Optimize Supplier Directories: Facilitate deep analytical filtering to allow managers to segment global suppliers dynamically by risk level and food safety standards.

Role

  • Owned end-to-end UX/UI design, translating complex supply chain matrices into highly readable command controls.
  • Designed the step-by-step AI-Assisted Query Generation Form alongside contextual side-by-side resolution tip sheets.
  • Crafted visual layout configurations for the Advanced Supplier Directory, incorporating deep sidebar filters and metadata status rows.
  • Engineered the layout structures for the Certificate Expiry Tracker and real-time SLA visual badges.
  • Conducted user testing with global supplier networks to ensure rapid, error-free onboarding and data submissions.

Target users

Enterprise Quality Assurance (QA) managers, global food manufacturers, procurement leads, compliance inspectors, and C-level executives overseeing highly regulated global food supply chains.

Research

Observed enterprise supply chains in real time, auditing traditional vendor communication logs, static directory archives, and manual audit histories. Conducted extensive interviews with enterprise QA directors and foreign supplier leads to locate critical delays, cognitive overload thresholds, and certification tracking friction during official audits.

Wrestling with massive vendor spreadsheets, delayed communication during lab exceptions, and expired vendor certificates during sudden regulatory audits.

Pain points

  • Scattered communication channels obscure crucial lab evidence and delay immediate quality threat resolutions.
  • Static spreadsheets hide geographical risk parameters and make it hard to spot regional deviation trends.
  • Manual expiration tracking for safety standards (HACCP, ISO, BRCGS) creates severe regulatory and liability blind spots.
  • Cluttered compliance forms increase onboarding friction and create layout fatigue for global supplier nodes.

Information architecture

A high-density navigation hierarchy built on three primary modules: the Executive Command Center (overall health KPIs, network heatmaps), the Supplier Quality Ledger (interactive directory, multi-dimensional filters, certificate tracker), and the Collaborative Query Lifecycle Workspace (stepped query builder, secure audit timelines).

Competitive analysis

Evaluated legacy ERP systems, rigid supply chain managers, and standalone audit spreadsheets. Found a clear market opening for real-time risk pattern indicators, automated certification expiration alerts, and integrated supplier-buyer messaging.

Design decisions

  • A left-hand multi-dimensional filtering panel allowing QA managers to segment suppliers instantly by category, risk tier, or certificates.
  • An integrated split-screen query workspace containing detailed descriptions on one side and a secure contextual chat module on the other.
  • A clean, vertical, stepped Query Form layout featuring a prominent real-time side panel for AI writing suggestions.
  • Bold, color-coded SLA countdown badges and status alerts to draw focus directly to high-priority safety breaches.

Wireframes

Drafted multi-column wireframes to accommodate dense supplier data. Tested different sidebar widths and tabular risk views to maximize cognitive scanning efficiency and visual breathing room on large enterprise displays.

Visual design

Clean, high-trust corporate system: crisp lines, ample negative space, and functional visual accents. Semantic colors are applied intentionally—highlighting high-risk zones, active warning alerts, and compliant certificates without overwhelming the layout.

Design system

Constructed an enterprise-grade UI pattern library consisting of responsive data tables, step status chains, contextual conversation panels, drag-and-drop evidence cards, and dynamic filter checkboxes to streamline developer implementation.

Prototype

Assembled a high-fidelity interactive flow to test the end-to-end journey of a quality deviation. Evaluated critical actions including form filling with AI assistance, supplier-buyer conversations, and viewing overall network metrics.

Accessibility

  • Full WCAG AAA color contrasts across all risk statuses, color-coded badges, and dashboard scores.
  • Accessible HTML landmarks and keyboard-navigable data grids designed for quick, mouse-free supplier filtering.
  • Clear Aria descriptions for dynamic live counters, countdown clocks, and progressive file-drop zones.
  • Fully responsive viewport adaptation supporting error-free navigation on tablet and mobile viewports.

Developer handoff

Provided modular frontend specifications covering active data tables, filter mechanics, multi-step progress controls, and dynamic chart parameters, encouraging smooth alignment during implementation sprints.

Challenges

  • Displaying dense risk metrics and compliance histories without causing visual fatigue or clutter.
  • Consolidating scattered communication steps into an intuitive, audit-ready chat timeline.
  • Persuading external, low-tech supplier personnel to accurately update high-priority food safety credentials.

Solutions

  • An Executive Command Center utilizing simplified, interactive heatmaps and aggregate status cards to show network health at a glance.
  • An immutable, chronological Timeline Log running alongside a context-rich supplier chat box.
  • An automated Certification Ledger with built-in email triggers and warning alerts sent weeks before credential expiries.

Results

  • An Advanced Supplier Directory that cuts data-discovery latency down through precise left-hand filters.
  • A Centralized Query Workspace keeping internal teams and global vendors perfectly aligned on active safety threats.
  • An automated Certification Ledger that successfully eliminates supply chain gaps due to expired vendor credentials.

Impact

SmartFood Safe replaces manual communication friction with clean, data-driven workflows, creating an immutable bridge between QA teams and global supplier networks. It eliminates spreadsheet fatigue, prevents sudden certification lapses, and ensures constant, audit-ready regulatory compliance.

Key learnings

  • Visual indicators (such as automated countdowns) drastically improve supplier response speeds compared to text-only requests.
  • Consolidating chat archives and attachments in a chronological timeline fosters collaborative relationships instead of defensive dispute loops.
  • Preventive alerts (like early certificate notifications) are far more effective at protecting supply chains than reactive recovery measures.

Future improvements

  • Deeper IoT integrations to automatically pull real-time cold-chain temperature readings and lab values straight into active queries.
  • AI-enabled translation matrices to ease cross-border supplier negotiations on global portals.
  • Interactive regional forecasting dashboards pointing out climate and agricultural risk patterns.
