Zyra Scribe AI

Empowering clinicians to focus on patients, not paperwork, through real-time ambient AI transcription and structured clinical summaries.

Overview

Zyra Scribe AI is an AI-assisted workspace that securely listens to patient encounters (both in-person and virtual), extracts essential medical facts, and automatically structures them into validated medical frameworks like SOAP notes or customized Clinical Notes. It streamlines administrative workflows, allowing physicians to review, edit, and export flawless documentation within seconds.

Problem

Healthcare professionals spend an average of two hours on Electronic Health Record (EHR) documentation for every one hour of direct patient care. This administrative burden is a leading driver of clinician burnout, localized data fragmentation, and decreased patient satisfaction during visits due to "screen-staring."

Goals

  • Empower clinicians to focus entirely on patients, not paperwork, through real-time ambient AI transcription.
  • Collect essential clinician credentials and securely onboard healthcare providers onto Zyra Scribe AI with minimal step-latency.
  • Provide frictionless re-entry into high-velocity clinical environments without security compromises.
  • Design immediate, safe recovery pathways if credentials are forgotten during hectic, back-to-back shifts.

Role

  • Owned end-to-end UX/UI, transitioning complex clinical workflows into visual healthcare patterns.
  • Constructed the credential authentication gateways and simplified user security entry points.
  • Engineered the ambient voice session interface, dynamic waveforms, and multi-pane workspace shells.
  • Built and tested interactive template-building engines and responsive visual SOAP note modules.
  • Delivered accessible frontend specifications and high-fidelity layouts mapped for EHR-compatible frameworks.

Target users

Healthcare practitioners, primary care physicians, and clinical specialists operating within fast-paced, high-velocity healthcare facilities who need immediate, reliable tools to record encounters and generate accurate summaries effortlessly.

Research

Observed clinical environments first-hand during active physician shifts. Audited the standard credential submission workflows and analyzed friction points that delayed clinical practitioners during high-stress patient care transitions. Conducted 1-on-1 interviews with doctors to target the specific causes of digital documentation fatigue.

Pain points

  • Lengthy credential screens increase entry latency when entering high-pressure wards.
  • Lack of visible recording states leaves clinicians unsure if conversations are accurately ingested.
  • Pre-made medical formats fail to adapt to specialized physician preferences and medical practices.
  • Forgotten credentials during busy shifts lock clinicians out of active patient queues.

Information architecture

A unified navigation structure linking the central clinical engine room (recording dashboards, microphones, live waveforms), the specialized template manager (custom layouts, community gallery, authoring screens), and the clinical settings panel for verification and user details.

Competitive analysis

Mapped existing medical dictation programs, transcription services, and EHR plugins. Identified vital gaps in secure mobile re-entry speeds, template adaptation flexibility, and custom text-mapping features for professional clinician workflows.

Design decisions

  • A clean, vertical authentication card centered over a soft light-purple abstract background with prominent purple CTA buttons.
  • Stylized clinical cross/plus icon located top-center to establish direct medical brand trustworthiness.
  • A split dual-pane dashboard containing the formatted SOAP Note preview on the right with navigation history on the left.
  • A dedicated template gallery showcasing modular, white layout cards with views tracking and search fields.

Wireframes

Drafted low-fidelity concepts targeting the clinical interface split. Evaluated balanced dual-pane workspaces displaying indexed menus beside active physical medical charts to optimize clinician data density and readability.

Visual design

Clean, focus-driven system: generous breathing room, high-contrast typography, and intuitive functional icons (microphones, waveforms, document paperclips). Accent elements guide clinicians straight toward primary actions without cognitive distraction.

Design system

Developed responsive, medical-grade web components, including input forms, interactive metadata fields, microphone states, and specialized checkboxes. Standardized text-mapping inputs ensure seamless developer handover and fast implementation.

Prototype

Assembled an interactive dashboard flow simulating live ambient clinical session workflows. Tested key paths including onboarding credentials, recording voice triggers, formatting custom layouts, and editing text expansions.

Accessibility

  • W3C-compliant color contrasts for all audio recording states and waveforms.
  • Large, responsive click-targets for high-pressure, rapid physical environments.
  • Logical focus order across clinical metadata input modules and edit sheets.
  • Secure alternate authentication routes optimized for screen-reader workflows.

Developer handoff

Delivered dynamic components outlining active recording micro-states, secure entry forms, and physical preview sheets. Held walk-throughs to coordinate real-time transcription rendering behaviors and token-based layout styling.

Challenges

  • Minimizing security-step latency during intense physician shifts.
  • Balancing extensive clinical information within a dual-pane workspace.
  • Providing flexible custom layout tools without overwhelming non-technical users.

Solutions

  • A secure, streamlined access point designed to return clinicians directly to their dashboards safely.
  • A split-pane screen highlighting active transcripts, demographic fields, and mic state indicators dynamically.
  • An all-in-one metadata authoring panel with clear title inputs, structural selectors, and text guidelines.

Results

  • Clinicians can explore pre-made templates in an organized gallery framework.
  • A clean text-mapping workbench allows instant setup of customized text-expansion rules.
  • Onboarding credential forms mapped to minimize step latency and security friction.

Impact

Zyra Scribe AI completely eliminates manual typing during live clinical consultations, boosting direct face-to-face patient engagement and significantly lowering administrative burnout.

Key learnings

  • A secure, fast re-entry gateway drastically reduces workflow stress during clinical shifts.
  • Visual indicators like active microphone waves provide important reassurance of systems processing.
  • Physicians value having editable template configurations over static generic notes.

Future improvements

  • Expanded community templates showing clinical usage metrics and tracking.
  • Deeper text-mapping tools targeting automated prescription and medication guidelines.
  • Adaptive workspace layouts matching local physical medical specialties automatically.
